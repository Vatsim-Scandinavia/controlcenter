# Using Themes

Control Center supports multiple visual themes to suit your preferences and environment.

## Changing Your Theme

### Step 1: Go to Settings
1. Click your name in the top-right corner
2. Select **Settings** from the dropdown menu

### Step 2: Choose Your Theme
Find the "Theme Preference" dropdown and select:

- **System Default** - Automatically matches your device's theme
- **Light** - Always use the light theme
- **Dark** - Always use the dark theme

### Step 3: Save
Click the **Save** button at the bottom of the page.

Your theme will change instantly!

## How System Theme Works

When you select "System Default", Control Center automatically detects your device's theme preference:

### macOS
- Follows **System Preferences > General > Appearance**
- Auto-switches with "Auto" mode based on time of day

### Windows
- Follows **Settings > Personalization > Colors > Choose your color**
- Can be set to light, dark, or automatic

### Linux
- Follows your desktop environment's theme setting
- Varies by DE (GNOME, KDE, etc.)

### The Smart Part
When your system theme changes (e.g., at sunset), Control Center automatically switches too - no page reload needed!

## Tips & Tricks

### Quick Theme Testing
Want to try different themes?
1. Change your theme in settings
2. Browse around the application
3. If you don't like it, come back and change it again
4. Your preference saves automatically when you click Save

### Battery Saving (OLED Screens)
If you have an OLED display (many modern phones, tablets, and laptops):
- Dark theme can significantly save battery life
- Black pixels on OLED are actually "off"
- Consider using dark theme when on battery power

### Eye Strain Reduction
If you experience eye strain:
- Try dark theme in low-light environments
- Use light theme in bright environments
- Let system theme switch automatically
- Consider taking regular breaks regardless of theme

### Accessibility
Both themes meet WCAG accessibility standards:
- Sufficient contrast ratios for all text
- Clear visual indicators for interactive elements
- Color is not the only means of conveying information

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Will my theme choice affect other users?
**A:** No, each user has their own theme preference. Your choice only affects what you see.

### Q: Does the theme change affect mobile?
**A:** Yes! Your theme preference works on all devices once you're logged in.

### Q: Can I schedule theme changes?
**A:** Use "System Default" - most operating systems can schedule light/dark mode based on time of day.

### Q: What if I don't see the theme option?
**A:** Contact your administrator. The theme system may need to be enabled or updated.

### Q: Does dark theme save data/bandwidth?
**A:** No, themes only change how the page looks. The data transferred is the same.

### Q: Can I customize the colors?
**A:** Individual users cannot customize colors, but administrators can. See the [administrator documentation](setup/theme.md).

### Q: Which theme is better?
**A:** It's personal preference! We recommend:
   - **Light** during the day
   - **Dark** in the evening
   - **System** to switch automatically

### Q: Why does my theme reset sometimes?
**A:** This shouldn't happen. If it does:
   1. Make sure you clicked "Save" after selecting your theme
   2. Check that your browser isn't blocking cookies/storage
   3. Contact your administrator if the problem persists

## Technical Notes

### Browser Requirements
Modern browsers support theme switching:
- Chrome/Edge (version 88+)
- Firefox (version 85+)
- Safari (version 14+)
- Opera (version 74+)

Older browsers will display the light theme only.

### Privacy
Your theme preference:
- Is stored in the database (when logged in)
- Is also stored locally in your browser for fast loading
- Does not track or collect any additional information
- Can be changed at any time

## Need Help?

If you experience any issues with themes:

1. **Try clearing your browser cache**
   - Chrome/Edge: Ctrl+Shift+Delete (Windows) or Cmd+Shift+Delete (Mac)
   - Firefox: Ctrl+Shift+Delete (Windows) or Cmd+Shift+Delete (Mac)
   - Safari: Cmd+Option+E (Mac)

2. **Try a different browser**
   - This helps identify if it's browser-specific

3. **Check with your administrator**
   - They can verify the theme system is properly configured

4. **Report the issue**
   - Include your browser name and version
   - Describe what you see vs. what you expect
   - Include screenshots if possible
